\section*{1.\quad Summary of Progress}

The proposal aimed to build a prototype pipeline that turns short campus walkway videos into \emph{motion-aware} assistive alerts for blind and low-vision pedestrians, by classifying each tracked object as one of six hazard categories: \texttt{approaching}, \texttt{crossing\_left\_to\_right}, \texttt{crossing\_right\_to\_left}, \texttt{moving\_away}, \texttt{static}, or \texttt{uncertain}. As of week~3 the full end-to-end system is implemented, tested on 11 campus videos I filmed, evaluated against a 61-track manually labeled ground-truth set, and instrumented with explainability metadata (every \texttt{uncertain} label carries a sub-reason such as \texttt{short\_track} or \texttt{near\_approaching}).

\textbf{Data:} The 11 videos (IMG\_4972--4982) are \emph{controlled} captures for labeling/ablations; the proposal's one \textbf{uncontrolled} natural campus walk is \textbf{not yet recorded} (planned day~5, qualitative demo only).

\subsection*{Pipeline overview}
\textbf{(1) Detection:} Ultralytics YOLO \texttt{v8n} on the COCO subset \{person, bicycle, motorcycle, skateboard\} with confidence threshold $0.35$.
\textbf{(2) Tracking:} per-class IoU\,+\,centroid matcher (weighted $0.7\cdot\text{IoU} + 0.3\cdot(1-\hat d)$) with a \texttt{max\_frame\_gap=2} buffer.
\textbf{(3) Motion features:} centroid trajectory, bounding-box growth ratio, Farneback optical flow (median dx/dy, magnitude), and frame-differencing overlap inside the bbox. Both flow and frame-diff are \emph{ego-motion compensated} by subtracting the global median flow and ECC-aligning consecutive frames (translation model).
\textbf{(4) Classifier:} a rule cascade over $\{\text{growth\_ratio}, \text{dx\_norm}, \text{approach\_score}\}$ that returns a hazard label plus a textual evidence string.
\textbf{(5) Alerts:} reason-aware natural-language messages (``Bike approaching from the left.''; for uncertain, ``Person detected briefly; not enough frames to judge motion.'').
\textbf{(6) Evaluation:} accuracy, per-class precision/recall/F1, and confusion matrix against \texttt{data/labels/hazard\_labels.csv}.

\subsection*{Numbers (11 videos, 61 labeled tracks)}
\textbf{Overall accuracy: 67.2\,\% (41/61); Macro F1: 0.478.} Per-class F1: \texttt{approaching} 0.82 ($n{=}24$), \texttt{moving\_away} 0.71 ($n{=}9$), \texttt{crossing\_right\_to\_left} 0.67 ($n{=}3$), \texttt{uncertain} 0.67 ($n{=}16$), \texttt{crossing\_left\_to\_right} 0.00 ($n{=}7$), \texttt{static} 0.00 ($n{=}2$). The \texttt{approaching} precision of 0.90 is the headline safety-relevant number: when the system says ``approaching'' it is correct 90\,\% of the time. Engineering is supported by a 78-test \texttt{pytest} suite covering motion estimation, classifier rules, alerts, evaluation, and the video writer.

\subsection*{Ablations conducted}
\textbf{(a) Classifier cascade v1 $\to$ v2.} After labeling all 61 tracks, I diagnosed two systematic failure modes in the original cascade (diagonal walkers being mis-labeled \texttt{approaching} despite dominant lateral motion; receding objects being mis-labeled \texttt{crossing}). v2 inserts a \texttt{strong\_crossing} branch (fires when $|\text{dx\_norm}|>0.50$) before \texttt{approaching}, and moves \texttt{moving\_away} before the crossing branches. \textbf{Result: 59.0\,\% $\to$ 67.2\,\% accuracy, Macro F1 0.302 $\to$ 0.478 (+58\,\% relative).}

\textbf{(b) ByteTrack drop-in (negative result).} I wired \texttt{supervision.ByteTrack} as an opt-in backend hoping it would reduce the 1--2 frame tracker fragments that drive most remaining errors. Even after tuning (\texttt{frame\_rate=2}, \texttt{lost\_track\_buffer=10}, \texttt{minimum\_matching\_threshold=0.95}), ByteTrack \textbf{dropped 51\,\% of detections} and scored only \textbf{32.8\,\% accuracy}. ByteTrack's Kalman-filter design is mismatched with low-fps offline footage; this is a useful negative finding for the final report.

\section*{3.\quad Deviations from the Proposal}

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Rule-based classifier instead of learned.} The proposal hedged between a learned classifier and a rule cascade. I committed to the rule cascade because (i) no labeled training set existed in week~1, (ii) rules give explicit \emph{evidence strings} the assistive alert layer can quote, and (iii) thresholds can be tuned against a small labeled set without overfitting a deep model.
\item \textbf{Ego-motion compensation added.} Not in the original plan; added in week~3 after observing that hand-held iPhone footage produced large false-positive flow magnitudes during camera pans. Implemented as median-flow subtraction + ECC alignment.
\item \textbf{ByteTrack tested but not adopted.} A planned tracker upgrade was implemented and benchmarked, then \emph{deliberately not used}---a negative result worth reporting honestly.
\item \textbf{Uncontrolled walk pending.} Controlled clips first (pipeline + 61-track eval); natural campus-walking video still required before final submission.
\item \textbf{Scope unchanged.} Hazard taxonomy, campus-walkway setting, and assistive-alert framing match the proposal.
\end{itemize}
